📋 Assignment 2 Instructions: Critical Reading Reflection

Due Date: 8-15 September 2025 | Weight: 30 marks

Word Count: 850 - 1000 words

Submission: Online via clickUP (Turnitin-enabled)

Overview and Purpose

This assignment is designed to develop your critical thinking, analytical reading, and academic writing skills. You are required to select one of the key themes identified from our class survey, choose two provided academic articles related to that theme, and write a critical reflection that analyses and synthesises the arguments presented in those texts.

Your response should not be a simple summary but a critical engagement with the ideas, arguments, and evidence presented by the authors.

Themes and Topics

Choose ONE of the following themes for your reflection:

  • Unemployment
  • Corruption
  • Entrepreneurship
  • National Dialogue & Governance
  • Education and Skills Mismatch
  • Economic Policies & Issues
  • Social Justice & Inequality

Writing Your Reflection

Your 850-1000 word reflection should analyse the articles by addressing:

  • Introduction: Briefly introduce the theme and state which two articles you have chosen. Provide a clear thesis statement.
  • Summary and Analysis: Succinctly summarise the core arguments of each article.
  • Synthesis and Critical Engagement: Compare and contrast the two articles. Critically evaluate their arguments and connect to the South African context.
  • Conclusion: Summarise your main analytical points.

Formatting and Referencing

  • File Format: .docx or .pdf
  • Font: Arial or Times New Roman, size ≤12
  • Spacing: 1.5
  • Referencing: Harvard style

📋 Assignment 3 Instructions: Team Argumentation Presentation

Due: 31 October | Weight: 30 marks | Format: In-class or Digital Narrative Presentation

Instructions

Choose ONE theme from Assignment 2 (Unemployment, Corruption, Entrepreneurship, etc.). Form a team of 3–5 students.

Develop a structured argumentative presentation taking a clear position on a debatable issue within your chosen theme.

Requirements

  1. Use the articles from Assignment 2 as your primary evidence.
  2. Cite sources using Harvard style (in-slide or in-narrative).
  3. Include at least one visual (graph, chart, or diagram) to support your argument; this can be GenAI.
  4. Acknowledge and refute one credible counterargument.
  5. Ensure equal contribution from all team members.

Format

  • Max 8-10 minutes presentation
  • Submit slides or digital narrative link via clickUP (Turnitin-enabled)
  • Font: Arial/Times New Roman, size ≤12 | Spacing: 1.5 (for any supporting doc)

Extra credit

Include x2 examples of meaningful and effective translanguaging that supports both the written text and the graphic or image.
